Razor Handle

This handle was a process of looking at ergonomics and accesability for all who could use it.
The model first went through numerous studies to determine a good ergonomic fit,
and was then casted using a smooth-cast hard plastic.

This design was developed with Maddie Fung for Des 301 at the University of Alberta

Sheltered Playground Design

Using the Queen Elizabeth Park in Vancouver, British Columbia as a backdrop, the idea of combining play
and shelter is important, especially when you consider Vancouver's typical rainy weather The playground
would effectively provide an area to play, and if inclement comes, provides an area of shelter for those
who may be caught in the affect area.

This design was developed for Des 303 at the University of Alberta

HomeHub Software

The HomeHub software idea was born out of the concept of brand agnosticism. While
major brands in today's IoT Smarthome market limit the products that can be used
with eachother, the idea of controlling all the of the pieces with a central software
that isn't locked to a manufacturer's compatability list just makes sense. Software would
be developed in modules, with each module enacting with a device's API on the network.

This design was developed for Des 401 at the University of Alberta
